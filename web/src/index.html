What this proves

  • The cleartext was signed by the holder of a specific Ed25519 private key, identified by the public key fingerprint shown above.
  • The signing key is bound to a particular device and was generated inside the KeyWitness keyboard.
  • The text has not been modified since it was signed — any alteration would invalidate the signature.
  • A keystroke biometrics hash was captured at signing time, providing an additional behavioral signal tied to the typist.

What this does NOT prove

  • Identity: The signature proves key possession, not who holds the key. You must independently verify that the public key belongs to the person you expect (standard PKI trust model).
  • Content truth: The text is proven authentic (it was really signed), but the claims within it may still be false.
  • Time accuracy: The timestamp is set by the signing device. A compromised device could backdate or forward-date it.
  • Biometric identity: The keystroke hash is a behavioral fingerprint, not a verified biometric identity.
